AI Case Helper ​

The AI Case Helper is Disclosurely's intelligent assistant for case analysis and investigation. Powered by advanced AI, it helps case handlers analyze reports faster, identify key issues, and make data-driven decisions.

What is the AI Case Helper? ​

The AI Case Helper is an AI-powered tool that:

  • Analyzes Case Content: Reviews report descriptions, messages, and notes
  • Identifies Key Issues: Extracts main concerns and themes
  • Provides Recommendations: Suggests next steps and actions
  • Detects Patterns: Identifies similarities with other cases
  • Assesses Risk: Evaluates severity and potential impact
  • Generates Summaries: Creates concise case overviews
  • Answers Questions: Responds to queries about cases
  • References Documents: Analyzes company policies and procedures

How It Works ​

AI Technology ​

The AI Case Helper uses DeepSeek AI, a state-of-the-art large language model trained to:

  • Understand workplace compliance issues
  • Recognize legal and regulatory concepts
  • Analyze organizational behavior
  • Identify risk factors
  • Provide evidence-based recommendations

Privacy & Security ​

Your data stays secure:

  • All analysis happens with encrypted data
  • AI cannot access data it's not authorized to see
  • No case data is used to train the AI model
  • Conversations are encrypted and private
  • AI complies with data residency requirements

What the AI can see:

  • Case details you explicitly share
  • Documents you upload for analysis
  • Your organization's uploaded policies
  • Public compliance best practices

What the AI cannot see:

  • Cases you don't share with it
  • Other organizations' data
  • Reporter identifying information
  • System configuration details

Using the AI Case Helper ​

Accessing the AI Helper ​

  1. Navigate to any case/report
  2. Click the "AI Helper" button in the top right
  3. The AI chat interface opens
  4. Start asking questions or request analysis

Alternatively:

  1. Go to Dashboard > AI Helper
  2. Select a case to analyze
  3. Start your conversation

Starting a Conversation ​

The AI Helper can assist with:

Case Analysis:

  • "Analyze this case and summarize the key issues"
  • "What are the main concerns in this report?"
  • "Identify potential policy violations"

Recommendations:

  • "What should I do next?"
  • "How should I proceed with this investigation?"
  • "What evidence should I collect?"

Pattern Recognition:

  • "Are there similar cases?"
  • "Has this issue happened before?"
  • "What patterns do you see?"

Risk Assessment:

  • "How serious is this issue?"
  • "What are the potential risks?"
  • "Should this be escalated?"

Policy References:

  • "What policies are relevant?"
  • "Does this violate company policy?"
  • "What do our procedures say about this?"

Key Features ​

1. Case Summarization ​

Get instant summaries of complex cases.

How to Use:

You: "Summarize this case"

AI Response Example:

CASE SUMMARY

Type: Harassment & Discrimination
Reported: March 15, 2024
Status: Investigating

KEY ISSUES:
• Employee reports age-based discrimination from manager
• Multiple incidents over 2-week period
• Public comments made in team meetings
• Passed over for promotion potentially due to age

PEOPLE INVOLVED:
• Reporter: 52-year-old employee
• Subject: Manager Sarah J.
• Witnesses: 3 team members identified

EVIDENCE:
• Specific quotes documented
• Multiple incidents with dates
• Witnesses present
• Promotion decision timeline

SEVERITY: High - potential legal liability

RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:
1. Interview witnesses immediately
2. Review promotion decision process
3. Check for other age discrimination patterns
4. Consider interim measures to separate parties

2. Investigation Recommendations ​

Get AI-powered suggestions for next steps.

How to Use:

You: "What should I investigate first?"

AI Response Example:

INVESTIGATION PRIORITIES

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS (Next 24-48 hours):
1. Interview the three witnesses named in the report
   - Schedule separately to avoid collusion
   - Ask about specific incidents mentioned
   - Document their recollections

2. Secure relevant evidence
   - Meeting recordings if available
   - Email communications
   - Promotion decision documentation
   - Performance reviews

3. Review promotion process
   - Criteria used for decision
   - All candidates considered
   - Decision timeline and rationale

NEXT STEPS (Week 1-2):
4. Interview the manager (Sarah J.)
   - Inform of investigation professionally
   - Ask about promotion decision
   - Inquire about management style

5. Check for pattern
   - Review previous complaints
   - Survey team anonymously if appropriate
   - Check HR records for similar issues

DOCUMENTATION:
• Keep detailed notes of all interviews
• Maintain chain of custody for evidence
• Document timeline of investigation
• Prepare interim report after witness interviews

RISK MITIGATION:
• Consider whether parties should be separated
• Ensure no retaliation against reporter
• Notify leadership if severity warrants

3. Pattern Detection ​

Identify trends and patterns across cases.

How to Use:

You: "Check for similar cases involving this manager"

AI Response Example:

PATTERN ANALYSIS

SIMILAR CASES FOUND: 2

CASE #1: DIS-B3C7D9E2 (6 months ago)
• Type: Workplace bullying
• Subject: Same manager (Sarah J.)
• Issue: Aggressive communication style
• Outcome: Verbal warning, management training
• Similarity: 85%

CASE #2: DIS-E4F8A1B5 (1 year ago)
• Type: Favoritism concerns
• Subject: Same manager (Sarah J.)
• Issue: Unequal treatment of team members
• Outcome: Coaching provided
• Similarity: 65%

PATTERN IDENTIFIED:
• Manager has history of problematic behavior
• Issues recurring despite previous interventions
• Pattern suggests more serious, systemic problem
• Training/coaching has not been effective

RECOMMENDATION:
This appears to be a serious pattern rather than an isolated incident. Consider:
• Escalating to HR leadership
• More serious disciplinary action
• Management skills assessment
• Potential reassignment or termination

4. Risk Assessment ​

Evaluate severity and potential impacts.

How to Use:

You: "Assess the risk level of this case"

AI Response Example:

RISK ASSESSMENT

OVERALL RISK LEVEL: HIGH

LEGAL RISK: HIGH
• Age discrimination is prohibited under employment law
• Multiple incidents strengthen potential claim
• Witnesses present make this highly credible
• Promotion denial adds significant weight
• Company may face EEOC complaint or lawsuit
• Previous similar complaints suggest pattern

REPUTATIONAL RISK: MEDIUM
• Could damage employer brand if public
• May affect employee morale if unresolved
• Social media risk if reporter goes public

FINANCIAL RISK: MEDIUM-HIGH
• Potential lawsuit costs
• Settlement or judgment
• EEOC investigation costs
• Lost productivity
• Recruitment/replacement costs

OPERATIONAL RISK: LOW-MEDIUM
• Team dynamics affected
• Management capability questioned
• Temporary disruption during investigation

COMPLIANCE RISK: HIGH
• Clear policy violation
• Potential regulatory violation
• Audit findings risk
• Documentation essential

MITIGATION PRIORITY: URGENT
• Act swiftly and decisively
• Document everything thoroughly
• Engage legal counsel if appropriate
• Take interim protective measures
• Monitor for retaliation

5. Document Analysis ​

Upload and analyze company documents for context.

How to Use:

  1. Click "Upload Document"
  2. Select policy, handbook, or procedure
  3. Ask AI to reference it

Example:

You: "Does this violate our harassment policy?"

AI: Based on your Anti-Harassment Policy (uploaded March 2024):

POLICY VIOLATION: YES

Your policy states:
"Harassment based on age, race, gender, or other protected characteristics is strictly prohibited. This includes verbal comments, jokes, or actions that create an intimidating or hostile work environment."

VIOLATIONS IDENTIFIED:
1. Age-based comments ("too old to understand technology")
2. Multiple incidents creating hostile environment
3. Adverse employment action (promotion denial)
4. Public nature of comments (team meetings)

RELEVANT POLICY SECTIONS:
• Section 3.2: Prohibited Conduct
• Section 3.4: Manager Responsibilities
• Section 5.1: Reporting and Investigation

REQUIRED ACTIONS PER POLICY:
• Immediate investigation (Section 6.1)
• Witness interviews (Section 6.3)
• Interim protective measures (Section 6.5)
• Resolution within 30 days (Section 6.8)
• Written outcome to reporter (Section 7.1)

• Severity: Higher (multiple witnesses)

TRENDS IDENTIFIED:
• 40% involve management (vs 25% last year)

BENCHMARK RECOMMENDATIONS:
Based on similar cases, typical outcomes include:
• Formal warning: 50%

Formal warning minimum, consider suspension

What-If Scenarios ​

Explore potential outcomes:

You: "What if we just give the manager more training?"

AI: SCENARIO ANALYSIS: Training-Only Response

PROS:
• Lower conflict/resistance
• Faster resolution
• Less disruptive
• Lower costs

CONS:
• Insufficient given pattern (2 prior complaints)
• May not prevent recurrence
• Could be seen as inadequate by reporter
• Doesn't address seriousness
• May encourage future violations
• Potential legal liability if pattern continues

RISK ASSESSMENT:
HIGH RISK - Training alone is likely insufficient

RECOMMENDATION:
Training should be part of response, not entire response.
Consider: Formal warning + training + probation + regular check-ins

SUPPORTING DATA:
• Cases with training-only after patterns: 60% recurrence
• Cases with progressive discipline: 15% recurrence
• Legal precedent favors stronger action for patterns

Best Practices ​

Do: ​

✅ Provide Context: Give AI complete case information ✅ Ask Specific Questions: More specific = better answers ✅ Review Recommendations: AI assists, humans decide ✅ Upload Policies: Help AI understand your organization ✅ Save Important Analysis: Download or screenshot key insights ✅ Iterate: Ask follow-up questions for clarity ✅ Cross-Reference: Verify AI suggestions with policies

Don't: ​

❌ Blindly Follow: AI is a tool, not a replacement for judgment ❌ Share Sensitive Data Unnecessarily: Only share what's needed ❌ Rely Solely on AI: Combine AI with human expertise ❌ Ignore Red Flags: If AI misses something obvious, note it ❌ Skip Documentation: Document your own analysis too

Limitations ​

The AI Case Helper is powerful but has limitations:

AI Cannot:

  • Replace human judgment and decision-making
  • Interview witnesses or gather physical evidence
  • Guarantee legal compliance or outcomes
  • Access information it hasn't been given
  • Understand your organization's specific culture fully
  • Make final decisions on disciplinary actions

AI May:

  • Occasionally misinterpret context
  • Make suggestions that need adaptation
  • Not know recent policy changes unless told
  • Miss nuances in complex situations

Always:

  • Verify AI recommendations
  • Apply organizational knowledge
  • Consult legal/HR when appropriate
  • Use professional judgment
  • Document your reasoning
